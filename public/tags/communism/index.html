Banking in the Soviet Union

There is perhaps no industry more quintessentially capitalist than banking. Bankers are widely derided by communists as a useless appendage of society, perpetuating and profiting from the corrupt capitalist system. However, while Marx envisioned a moneyless society, it is true that socialist states in the 20th century never quite achieved their utopian vision of a post-money world. So, I wondered what banking would look like in a socialist state, such as the U.S.S.R.
